Moment: Informing Drivers at Interruptible Moments


Duration: 3 months
Research Advisor: Seungjun Kim, Anind Dey 
Project Brief: To design intelligent information systems that enable drivers to naturally and effectively receive information in cars.
My Role: Speech Interaction Design, Literature Review, UI Design, Video Sketching


Driving is a task  that takes up high cognitive load. Often times, drivers experience difficulty taking in information other than the ones relative to driving simply because they can't pay attention to them.

Assisting Dr.Seungjun Kim at Ubiquitous Computing Lab,  I designed and simulated an intelligent in-car  navigation system that informs drivers at interruptible moments. The types of information are not limited to the directions but also include contextual and non-contextual notifications that the driver might find useful. 

The goal of this research is to discover the best method to capture driver's attention  and successfully deliver information.  Our hypothesis is that if information is presented at moments where the driver's cognitive load is low, it would be easier for the driver to absorb the information. Another hypothesis is that if more than one type of feedback methods are used, it would help capture the driver's attention better.  So the current system is designed to present all visual, auditory, and haptic feedbacks to the driver at interruptible moments. 

The research is still at its testing phase - so I was asked to design three different versions of the system with different lengths and complexities of information.  For short interruptible moments, 1 - 2 information pieces would be presented in brief audio description  and for long interruptible moments, 2 - 3 information pieces would be presented in detailed description.
